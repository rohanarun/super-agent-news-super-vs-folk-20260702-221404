Why computer-use agents matter now

Benchmarks expose limits

Independent testing shows that many computer-use agents still struggle with real-world workflows, especially authentication and multi-step tasks. Completion rates drop sharply on login flows, underscoring how hard real computer use remains [theeditorial.news].

The big platforms are moving

Google recently made computer use a first-class capability inside Gemini 3.5 Flash, signalling that browser and desktop control is becoming central to AI agents [rohitraj.tech] [blog.google].

Cost and reliability tension

Most agents today incur similar costs every run and require supervision. Super’s approach — caching successful computer actions — aims to change the economics for repeatable workflows [digg.com].
